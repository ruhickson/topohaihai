South Park
==========

Description
-----------
First-person shooter based on the TV series: play as Cartman, Kyle, Stan, or Kenny with crude weapons (e.g., snowballs, dodgeballs) through a campaign of absurdist missions and multiplayer options. Voice clips and humor match the show’s tone.

Genre: First-person shooter / comedy

Developer: Iguana Entertainment (Acclaim Studios Austin lineage)

Publisher: Acclaim Entertainment

Platform: Nintendo 64

Release dates (retail)
----------------------
• North America: December 21, 1998  
• Europe: typically 1999 (often cited May 1999 for PAL)  

Ratings / certification
-------------------------
• ESRB: M (Mature) — US (language, crude humor)  

Other notes
-----------
• Also on PC and PlayStation; N64 version is a notable “adult” licensed title on the system.  
• Verify exact EU date on regional packaging—PAL street dates vary by country.

Thoughts
--------
South Park on N64 is basically a shooter with a license duct-taped on top, and sometimes that’s funny, sometimes it’s just crude for crude’s sake. Highlights: if you were a teenager when this dropped, the voices and jokes land like comfort food—turkeys, snowballs, dumb weapons, Kyle yelling. It can be a guilty laugh for an hour. Lowlights: the gameplay itself is pretty mid—repetitive corridors, mushy aiming, and a general sense that the joke carried the product more than level design did.

I’m not mad at it culturally, but replaying it now is rough: humor ages weirdly, and the “shock” stuff feels less shocking and more tiring. Compared to GoldenEye on the same console, the gunplay feels like a different species entirely—more arcade-ish, less satisfying. Multiplayer might entertain for a bit if everyone’s in a silly mood, but it’s not a staple.

Part of the problem is expectations: if you want tight shooter design, you’ll bounce; if you want a gag delivery system, you might tolerate it longer. I fall into the first camp—I laugh once, then I want gameplay to carry me, and it doesn’t. It’s basically a novelty record: fun at a party, awkward alone.

The weapons are silly on purpose—turkeys, fart jokes, whatever—but novelty wears thin when levels feel samey and you’re hunting for the exit like it’s a chore. I’m glad it exists as a cultural artifact; I just don’t want to live there.

Voice clips repeat, jokes land once, and then you’re basically doing chores with swearing—fine for a rental in 1998, rough as a “classic” today.

If you’re hunting “good FPS on N64,” this isn’t where you stop—you keep walking until you hit Rare’s output and maybe *Doom 64.* Final thought: one thumb down for me as a game I’d recommend today—funny memory, not a great time machine.

**Verdict:** 👎 (one thumb down)
