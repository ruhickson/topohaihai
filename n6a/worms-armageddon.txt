Worms Armageddon
================

Description
-----------
Turn-based artillery tactics: teams of worms fight across destructible 2D landscapes using outrageous weapons. The N64 port adapts the PC hit with multiplayer support and speech banks; humor and strategy are central.

Genre: Turn-based tactics / artillery

Developer: Team17

Publisher: Infogrames (European N64 publishing commonly cited; regional branding may vary)

Platform: Nintendo 64

Release dates (retail)
----------------------
• Europe/Australia: often cited December 11, 1999 (verify on your PAL packaging)  
• North America: March 23, 2000  

Ratings / certification
-------------------------
• ELSPA / USK / etc. on PAL packaging (era-appropriate); PEGI not used at original N64 launch in many EU markets.  
• ESRB: E (Everyone) — typical US release when applicable  

Other notes
-----------
• PC version is the most feature-rich; N64 port had platform-specific compromises.  
• Part of the long-running *Worms* series.

Thoughts
--------
Worms on N64 is a weirdly perfect fit for the console’s social side: turn-based chaos, trash talk between rounds, and that “one bad grenade ruins everything” energy. Highlights: the humor lands if you like British comedy and silly voices, and matches can swing wildly which keeps them from feeling stale. Lowlights: it’s not the best version technically—load times, UI compromises, and missing PC bells and whistles show up if you know what you’re missing.

I still think it’s a blast in the right group: you’re not playing for graphics, you’re playing for psychological warfare and accidental self-knockouts. Some weapons feel unfair in the best way, others feel unfair in the annoying way—classic Worms. If you’re solo, it’s fine, but you’re not getting the full magic without humans beside you.

The N64 controller’s weirdness actually fits turn-based chaos: you’re not doing fast-twitch FPS; you’re aiming, giggling, and panicking. That said, the port sacrifices some convenience and content compared to PC, so purists will complain—and they’re not wrong, just picky.

Holy hand grenades, concrete donkeys, prod pushes—if you know, you know. The joy is in the table-flip moments where one shot undoes five minutes of careful positioning.

Wind and terrain RNG can make serious plans look stupid, which is either hilarious or unfair depending on whether you’re winning.

Voice packs and one-liners are half the soundtrack to a good session—if you’re not quoting worms at each other, you’re doing it wrong.

Longer matches turn into diplomacy: fragile truces, sudden betrayals, and someone always “accidentally” drops a banana in the worst spot.

By the end, nobody remembers the score—only the clip where someone blew themselves up with style.

Grab snacks, pass the controller, and don’t take it seriously—that’s the whole point. Final thought: one thumb up on N64 specifically—great party potential, but I’d pick PC for a purist experience.

**Verdict:** 👍 (one thumb up)
